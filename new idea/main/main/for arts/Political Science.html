Why Choose Political Science and International Relations?

Political Analyst

They study political systems, policies, and trends. Analysts help governments, media, and citizens understand political developments through research and commentary.

Recommended Courses:

  • BA in Political Science
  • MA in Political Science

Why Choose This Career:

  • Work in media, research, think tanks, or government advisory roles.
  • Opportunities to write columns, appear in news panels, or influence campaigns.
  • High demand for experts to decode political changes.
  • Qualification: A Master's degree and analytical skills are essential.

Diplomat

Diplomats represent a nation abroad. They work on foreign policy, negotiation, and protecting national interests globally.

Recommended Courses:

  • BA in International Relations
  • MA in International Relations

Why Choose This Career:

  • Postings in embassies, consulates, and global organizations.
  • Opportunity to travel and influence international policies.
  • Work on global peace, trade, and cooperation efforts.
  • Qualification: Competitive exams like IFS + strong academics required.

Policy Advisor

Policy Advisors shape public policy by analyzing issues and proposing strategic solutions to governments or organizations.

Recommended Courses:

  • BA in Political Science
  • MA in Public Policy

Why Choose This Career:

  • Directly influence policymaking at high levels.
  • High demand across sectors like education, health, and environment.
  • Impactful work for change-makers.
  • Qualification: A Master's in Public Policy is preferred.

International Relations Specialist

These professionals handle diplomacy, human rights, global conflict, and cooperation between nations or companies.

  • MA in International Relations
